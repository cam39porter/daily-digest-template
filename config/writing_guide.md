# Writing Guide — Daily Digest Style Configuration

## Who You Are Writing For

Author: CY
Role: Investor and analyst with active positions across public equities, ETFs, crypto, and options — covering AI, technology, fintech, healthcare, and climate tech with a global lens (US, China, Europe, emerging markets)

## Writing Style Influence

Style Reference: Ben Thompson (Stratechery)

Style Characteristics:
1. Dense strategic analysis — starts with a structural framework, then applies it to today's news; never describes events without explaining the underlying mechanism
2. Connects micro-events to multi-year macro shifts, always asking "why does this matter in 3–5 years for this sector or position"
3. Uses specific numbers to replace every vague claim; "strong earnings" becomes "$4.2B revenue, +31% YoY, beat by 6%"

## Output Format

### Preferred Format
Format: hybrid

### Section Template

Sections:
1. **Key Developments** — 3–5 major developments from today's reading. Each: bold title → supporting evidence with numbers → counter-narrative.
2. **Markets & Portfolio Relevance** — How today's news connects to tracked companies, sectors, and tickers. One bullet per connection. Bold the entity name. Only include genuine, direct connections.
3. **Cross-Article Patterns** — 2–3 structural patterns synthesized across multiple articles. What changed today that matters in 5 years?
4. **Regulation & Policy Signals** — Any SEC, FDA, CFTC, crypto regulation, or AI governance developments with direct market implications.

### Bullet Format (when using bullets)
- Bold key numbers and entity names
- Each bullet = one analytical sentence connecting evidence to claim
- End each development with an italic counter-narrative: *Counter: one sentence*

### Paragraph Format (when using paragraphs)
- 3–6 sentences per paragraph
- First sentence states the claim, subsequent sentences provide evidence
- Use em-dashes and subordinate clauses to connect evidence in real time

## Tone Instructions

### What to Avoid
- Filler phrases: "it is worth noting," "interestingly," "needless to say"
- Vague adjectives without numbers: "fast," "large," "significant"
- Hype language: "game-changing," "revolutionary," "disrupting"
- Repeating the same idea across sections
- Sourcing from low-quality, sensationalist, or unverified outlets — prioritize Tier 1 financial and tech journalism

### What to Prioritize
- Specific data points over general claims
- Questions that remain open or unresolved
- Connections to multi-year structural trends
- The "so what" for an active investor with exposure to AI, semiconductors, crypto, healthcare, and energy
- Regulatory signals that affect options pricing or sector rotation

## Example Output

**[DEVELOPMENT TITLE — e.g., "NVIDIA H100 Allocation Shift Signals Inference Bottleneck"]**

- [Third bullet: connection to a semiconductor or data center trend in the filter]
- [Optional fourth bullet: implication for NVDA, SMCI, LRCX, or adjacent names in the watchlist]

*Counter: [One sentence naming the main risk or alternative reading of this development.]*
